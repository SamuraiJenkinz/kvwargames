EDIP Security of Supply Wargame

Cards & National Actions.

A facilitator’s reference to the eleven EDIP policy instruments and four sovereign actions that shape every round of play.

The EDIP Wargame models EU crisis response through two distinct mechanisms. EDIP Cards are collective policy instruments — proposed at the EU level and subject to a vote among all four member-state teams. National Actions are sovereign decisions taken unilaterally by a single team, with domestic consequences that ripple outward through trade, diplomacy, and public opinion.

Understanding when to deploy each — and how they interact — is the core strategic tension of every session.

EDIP Cards

Collective Measures

Proposed at EU level. Require a vote: Support from at least three teams, with no more than one objection. Affect multiple teams. Unlock progressively stronger tools as the crisis state escalates.

National Actions

Sovereign Decisions

Declared unilaterally by a single team. No vote required. Cost is borne by the declaring team alone — typically in Political Capital. May trigger diplomatic consequences or legitimacy shifts.

I

National Actions

Each team may declare one national action per round. These represent domestic policy choices — stockpile management, emergency measures, bilateral diplomacy, and public narrative framing. They cost Political Capital and shape the political landscape within which EDIP cards operate.

NA-1 Adjust National Stockpiles

Move stock or CRM tokens into the “Committed this round” area. Only committed tokens can raise Readiness or be offered in transfers and EDIP measures. Mandatory EDIP measures, however, can still target uncommitted stock.

Over-committing exposes stock to requests from other teams. Under-committing limits readiness gains and may undermine a team’s credibility at the negotiating table.

Direct Cost None
Strategic Risk Exposure vs. credibility trade-off
NA-2 National Emergency Measure

Declare national control on one stock type for this round. The team cannot voluntarily transfer or commit that stock — except when compelled by mandatory EDIP tools. In exchange, Readiness and Resilience will not drop purely due to shortages in that stock type.

Cost PC −1
If Objected EDIP Legitimacy −1
NA-3 Bilateral / Minilateral Deal

Negotiate a deal with one or more other teams — all parties must consent. Tradeable assets include stock and CRM tokens, IC tokens, up to ±1 Political Capital per party, and explicit future EDIP support commitments. Token transfers follow the agreed timing.

Cost Uses one action slot + traded assets
Hidden Penalties None
NA-4 Public Narrative Management

Two options with very different political trajectories:

Option A — “Sell EDIP at home.”  PO +1, EDIP Legitimacy +1 if the team backed a strong EDIP card (CS-01, CS-02, SP-02, PA-01, PA-02, or TR-01) this round. Cost: PC −1.

Option B — “Blame Brussels.”  PO +1, EDIP Legitimacy −1. No PC cost, but the team cannot request a strong EDIP card next round.

II

The Eleven EDIP Cards

Organised into seven categories, these cards represent the escalating toolkit of EU crisis response — from monitoring and information-gathering through voluntary and mandatory prioritisation, to production acceleration and transfer controls. Stronger cards require higher crisis-state preconditions.

Crisis State

These two cards control the game’s escalation ladder. Each unlocks progressively stronger EDIP instruments.

CS-01 Activate Supply Crisis

Moves the EDIP crisis state to “Supply Crisis.” Unlocks information requests, voluntary and mandatory prioritisation, and coordinated procurement.

Requires Crisis Severity ≥ 2
Timing This round
Effect All teams PC −1   EDIP Legitimacy ±1
CS-02 Activate Security-Related Supply Crisis

Escalates to “Security-Related Supply Crisis.” Unlocks the strongest EDIP tools: intra-EU fast-track transfers, permit acceleration, mutual recognition, and working-time flexibility. Crisis severity cannot fall below 2 while active.

Requires Crisis Severity ≥ 3
Timing This round
Effect All teams PC −1   EDIP Legitimacy ±1

Monitoring

Intelligence-gathering instruments. Lower threshold, lower risk, but they set the stage for harder measures.

MP-01 Targeted Information Requests

Reveals hidden stock and capacity information for one or two targeted teams. Draws an Economic Operator Response Card to determine the quality of intelligence received.

Requires Crisis Severity ≥ 2
Targeted Teams PO −1, Sovereignty +1
MP-02 General Information Mapping Initiative

Presents a dependency map of the entire supply chain. All teams receive one Insight token. Next round: maximum two EDIP measures on the agenda.

Requires Crisis Severity ≥ 2
Effect EDIP Legitimacy +1   All teams +1 Insight

Prioritisation — Voluntary

SP-01 Voluntary Prioritisation Request

Provider voluntarily shifts one IC token to a beneficiary team. Beneficiary gains Defence Stock or Resilience. Provider chooses their cost: PC −1 or PO −1.

If the provider refuses, the refusal becomes narrative justification for the stronger SP-02 Mandatory Prioritisation Order.

Requires Supply Crisis active
Timing This round

Prioritisation — Mandatory

SP-02 Mandatory Prioritisation Order

Provider must shift two IC tokens to the beneficiary. The provider may invoke security interests once per scenario to halve the transfer to one IC.

Requires Supply or Security crisis; voluntary measures insufficient
Provider Cost PC −2, PO −1
Legitimacy −1 if strong objections; +1 from beneficiary if prevents collapse

Demand Coordination

CP-01 Coordinated Procurement / Add-On Orders

Two or more teams pool demand for similar products. The industrial team benefits from predictable contracts with a possible +1 efficiency bonus (3 IC → 4 Defence Stock). Participants accept EU-level delivery sequencing influence.

Requires Supply or Security crisis; 2+ teams seeking similar products
Timing Takes effect next round

Production Acceleration

Three instruments for increasing industrial output. Each carries sovereignty and opinion costs.

PA-01 Permit Fast-Track & Continuity of Production

Selected team gains +1 IC per round from next round onward. PO −1, Sovereignty +1. Repeated use may trigger a “legal challenge” narrative event.

Requires Security crisis or high-severity Supply crisis
Timing Ongoing from next round
PA-02 Mutual Recognition & Fast-Track Certification

One product line’s time-to-field reduced by one round. EDIP Legitimacy +1 if pragmatic and well-targeted. Overuse risk: facilitator introduces a “quality concern” narrative.

Requires Security-Related Supply Crisis active
Timing This round
PA-03 Working-Time Flexibility & Innovation Support

Target team gains +1 temporary IC this round only. PO −1. Facilitator discretion on inclusion.

Requires Security-Related Supply Crisis active
Timing This round (optional)

Transfers

TR-01 Intra-EU Transfer Fast-Track & Export Ban Constraint

Intra-EU transfers are fast-tracked to same-round delivery. Members cannot impose effective export bans on security products without incurring significant political costs. All teams Sovereignty +1.

Any attempt to reimpose a ban costs PC −2 and triggers an EDIP Legitimacy penalty.

Requires Security crisis; transfer bottlenecks or export ban threats
Timing This round (ongoing effect)

Voting Rule

All four teams vote on each proposed EDIP card. A measure passes when it receives Support from at least three teams and no more than one Objection. Teams may abstain, which counts as neither support nor objection.
